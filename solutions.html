PAST SOLUTIONS

for audio detection/classification.

HEARING AIDS

In-the-canal /completely-in-the-canal aids

  • Contained in tiny cases that fit partly/completely into the ear canal.
  • Their small size may make them difficult to handle/adjust.

Analog Hearing Aids

  • Increases volume of speech AND background noise. Its sound filters are limited so EVERY sound is heard.
  • Background noises will not be heard clearly as speech/other noises will be interfering.

Digital Hearing Aids

  • Converts sound waves into digital signals and produces a duplication.
  • Uses directional microphones to selectively detect voices and background sounds. May reduce background noise.
  • Reducing background noise has the opposite intention if one wants to hear background sounds more clearly.
  • Due to their unique features, digital ones are more expensive than analog.

Cons of Hearing Aids

  • Can cost up to $8,000, making them inaccessible to low-income users.
  • Designed to magnify human voices, not background noises, and they amplify/reduce background noise and speech at the same time.
  • Ear wax can block sound outlet and microphone can become damaged by moisture. Because a hearing aid is a physical object, its performance will always deteriorate.

MACHINE LEARNING SOLUTIONS

CNN (Convolutional Neural Network) Models

  • Contain a kernel that scans portions of audio and extracts features for identifying a specific sound correctly.
  • Doesn’t record location of audio features, which can reduce accuracy of audio identification.
  • In 2018, one proposed CNN, when trained on an audio dataset with augmentations+mixup techniques, increases classification accuracy to 83.7%.
    • Augmentations - add more variety to the dataset by changing the pitch/length of audio files. Can help a machine learning model learn edge cases and patterns of sounds better.
    • Mixup - two audio files in the dataset randomly combined as a new training sample. If the two files are from different sound categories, the resulting sound will become unusable and jumbled/messed up.
  • In 2020, a proposed Ensemble CNN using the already-made ResNet model reached 87.35% accuracy. However, ResNet contains over 11 million parameters, making this bulky model unable to be deployed into a small mobile app for consumer use.

Transformer-based Algorithms

  • Establishes long range relationships between input data, making the model understand each audio category’s distinctive features.
  • Require expensive computation and long epochs to train, which will take up a lot of RAM/memory on one's device. Will cost extra to purchase more GPUs to train model faster.
  • In 2021, the AudioCLIP model was proposed, which achieved 90.07% accuracy on an audio dataset. However, it also is combined with the ESResNeXt model, which contains 30 million parameters, too big to deploy.

Cons of Many Custom Machine Learning Algorithms

  • Too large to be deployed
  • High overhead costs (due to purchasing more GPUs to run the model faster)
  • Requires more time to develop and deploy ( more steps to deploy outside custom model into app)
