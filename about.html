ABOUT OUR SOLUTION

Audiority is a machine learning mobile app developed specifically to detect outdoor background noises and notify hearing impaired users.

It uses the latest technology and is free, accessible, and accurate.

Watch Our Demo Video

WHAT AUDIORITY DOES

Detects Outdoor Sounds

Whether it's a moving vehicle or ambulance or an armed gunman, Audiority can help hearing impaired users who are walking, driving, or biking move out of the way safely by utilizing their phones' microphones to pick up on these sounds - car horn, ambulance siren, and gunshot.

Displays Sounds on Screen

After detection, Audiority outputs the name of the sound directly on the screen. In addition, a picture representation of the sound is displayed in case English is not the user's main language.

Records History of Sounds Detected

Audiority also gives the user an option to record the history of the sounds detected. Users can see which sounds occurred at which exact time and date. This is important in case the user wants to report an accident, such as a car crash, or file a police report, such as for an armed person.

HOW AUDIORITY IS BUILT

CreateML

Apple framework I used for building, training, and optimizing Audiority's Audio Classification ML Model. It contains a custom convolutional neural network and is small enough (has a smaller number of parameters) to be deployed into the iOS App. In addition, I used the Kaggle dataset UrbanSound8K to train my model. The audio dataset is in the .wav format, including 929 siren, 429 horn, and 374 gunshot sounds. These sounds pose the most danger to people outside.

Swift

Apple's language I used to code the UI of the app (buttons, colors, words, etc).

XCode

Apple IDE I used to write Swift code in.

BENEFITS OF AUDIORITY

It's Free & Built Conveniently.

Because Audiority was developed with free resources/software, it will be available on the App Store as a free mobile app. Users only need to have an iPhone (1 billion users worldwide already own one). Because Audiority is built just using Apple's frameworks, integration, design, and deployment of the ML model is fast and easy.

It's accessible & convenient to use.

With just 3 buttons to press, Audiority is very easy to use. Users just need to press the start/stop recording button to have their phone's microphone start/stop detecting sounds. Second, users can press the check/clear history button to record the time/date of a sound detected. Lastly, users can choose to have their phone detect one, two, or all 3 of the sounds at once.

It's accurate, reliable, & lasts forever.

My trained, edited, and optimized audio model can detect and classify siren, horn, and gunshot sounds with a 90% accuracy every time. I have optimized the model enough that it could detect and output a sound category within 1 second. And because Audiority is an online app, it will not get damaged/deteriorate like a physical object (hearing aid) would.
